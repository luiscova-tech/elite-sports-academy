# Vision Statement

Elite Sports Academy is a sports management game with idle progression where players build a small local training center into the world's greatest Olympic training academy.

Players should never feel like they are simply accumulating numbers.

They should feel like they are:

- Building facilities
- Developing athletes
- Growing coaches
- Traveling the world
- Winning championships
- Producing Olympic champions

Every gameplay system should reinforce that fantasy.

## Five Pillars

- Build
- Develop
- Compete
- Progress
- Feel Proud

## Core Design Rule

If a feature does not strengthen the fantasy of running the world's greatest Olympic training academy, it should not be implemented.

## Development Philosophy

Every milestone should improve one of:

- Gameplay
- Experience
- Emotion

## Current Identity Direction

Academy Identity 1.0 makes the academy feel owned by the player through academy name, motto, crest, color theme, profile history, reputation rank, and a Hall of Fame foundation.

The identity layer is emotional and presentational first. It should not change economy, progression speed, competition systems, or country availability unless a later milestone explicitly approves that direction.

## Current First-Time Experience Direction

Academy Orientation makes the first minutes feel like taking ownership of a real academy. The player should immediately understand that this is their training center, Session Drill begins daily work, the Mission Card explains the current country chapter, and the World Tour shows what destination comes next.

The opening experience should be friendly, coach-led, compact, skippable, and practical. It should guide the player into training, upgrading, recruiting, competing, and traveling without adding separate tutorial-only mechanics.

Players should make real identity choices during orientation. Choosing an academy name, motto, crest, and color theme supports ownership more strongly than simply confirming a default profile.

Guidance should point the player toward the correct academy area and let them take the action themselves. A good recommendation feels like a coach saying where to go next, not the game playing itself.

Future onboarding changes should prioritize gameplay, experience, and emotion before account systems, usernames, leaderboards, or social features.

## Current Quality-of-Life Direction

Quality-of-life work should make the existing academy fantasy easier to read before adding more systems. Drill progression, coach growth, and competition outcomes should all feel understandable, rewarding, and connected to the academy story.

## Current Visual Identity Direction

Visual Identity 1.0 moves the game from prototype UI toward a handcrafted mobile sports management experience. The priority is making every screen feel like a department inside one living academy.

Home should welcome the player as Coach, make the current mission feel ceremonial, and keep Session Drill as the hero interaction. Sports, Academy, Competitions, and World should each use distinct visual language so the player feels they are moving through an academy, not browsing identical panels.

This direction is presentation-only. It should not change save data, economy, countries, athlete systems, competition systems, or progression pacing.

## Current World Tour Direction

World Tour 3.0 defines the long-term global academy architecture in `WORLD_TOUR.md`.

Elite Sports Academy is not an idle clicker and not a sequence of disposable country resets. It is a sports management game with idle progression where the player owns one global academy that expands around the world through permanent country campuses.

The academy's identity, reputation, medals, trophies, rank, Hall of Fame, and future global systems belong to the global academy forever. Each country campus should eventually own its own Training Points, facilities, athletes, competitions, buildings, local progress, visual theme, chapter progress, and country bonus.

Future country work should preserve completed countries as playable branches. Brazil and later destinations should remain preview-only until their implementation supports the global academy plus local campus model.
