# Purpose

Competitions are the primary way players measure the success of their academy.

They provide purpose for training, athlete development, coach management, and international expansion.

Competitions should feel like meaningful sporting events rather than simple reward generators.

---

# Design Philosophy

Players do not compete simply to earn Training Points.

Players compete to build the reputation of their academy.

Competitions create stories.

Winning should feel exciting.

Losing should still move the academy forward.

---

# Competition Levels

Elite Sports Academy contains several competition tiers.

## Local Competitions

Campus-level events.

Examples:

City Championships

Regional Invitationals

Youth Meets

Development Events

Purpose:

Learn

Train

Develop athletes

Discover talent

---

## National Championships

Every campus eventually competes nationally.

Examples:

USA Nationals

Japan Nationals

Brazil Nationals

Purpose:

Become national champion.

Unlock regional qualification.

Increase reputation.

---

## Regional Championships

Each world region has its own championship.

Examples:

North America & Caribbean Championships

South American Games

European Championships

Asian Games

African Games

Oceania Championships

Purpose:

Represent the academy internationally.

Earn major academy rewards.

Unlock World Championship qualification.

---

## World Championships

The best campuses from every region compete.

Purpose:

International prestige.

Major sponsors.

Academy reputation.

Olympic qualification.

---

## Olympic Games

The highest achievement in the game.

Olympic qualification should be difficult.

Not every athlete reaches the Olympics.

Winning Olympic medals becomes part of academy history.

Olympics should feel like the culmination of years of development.

---
