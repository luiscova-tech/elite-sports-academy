# Purpose

Defines the first-time player experience and onboarding flow.

The tutorial should teach through gameplay rather than large blocks of text.

---

# Tutorial Goals

By the end of the tutorial, the player should understand:

- Training athletes
- Hiring coaches
- Upgrading buildings
- Earning Training Points
- Winning competitions
- Unlocking new campuses

---

# Step 1 - Welcome

Introduce the player as the new Academy Director.

Reward:

- Starting Training Points
- First Coach
- First Athlete

---

# Step 2 - First Training Session

Guide the player to begin training.

Teach:

- Training Queue
- Progress Bar
- Session Rewards

---

# Step 3 - First Building Upgrade

Guide the player to upgrade the Training Center.

Teach:

- Upgrade Cost
- Upgrade Benefits

---

# Step 4 - First Coach

Guide the player to hire a coach.

Teach:

- Coach Bonuses
- Coach Levels

---

# Step 5 - First Competition

Enter a Local Competition.

Teach:

- Competition Rewards
- Reputation
- Medals

---

# Step 6 - Facility Expansion

Upgrade a second building.

Teach:

- Long-term investment
- Upgrade priorities

---

# Step 7 - Campus Complete

Reach the first campus milestone.

Unlock:

- World Map
- Japan

---

# Tutorial Rules

- Never show more than one new mechanic at a time.
- Allow players to skip dialogue.
- Highlight required UI elements.
- Keep explanations under two sentences.
- Reward every completed tutorial step.

---

# Playtest Improvements

Future improvements should include:

- Explain why Session Drills increase Training Speed.
- Explain Championship Trials before the first entry.
- Clearly distinguish Training Points from Academy Funding.
- Introduce Headquarters before the second campus.
- Explain offline progression and Operations Capacity.

---

# Design Philosophy

The tutorial should make players excited to continue, not eager to finish it.
