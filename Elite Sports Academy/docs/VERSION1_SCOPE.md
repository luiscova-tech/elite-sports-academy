# Purpose

This document defines the scope, goals, and guiding principles for Version 1 of Elite Sports Academy.

It serves as the executive summary of the project and should be read before any detailed design documents.

---

# Vision

Elite Sports Academy is a global sports management game where players build an international academy, expand into new countries, develop athletes, recruit coaches, compete in regional and world events, and ultimately pursue Olympic greatness.

The objective is not simply to build facilities.

The objective is to build the world's greatest sports academy.

---

# Version 1 Goals

Create a complete management experience centered around:

- Building international campuses
- Developing athletes
- Recruiting coaches
- Expanding worldwide
- Winning major competitions
- Growing Headquarters
- Becoming the world's most respected academy

---

# Core Design Principles

## One Global Academy

The player owns a single academy.

Every country becomes a permanent campus.

---

## Regions

Regions determine where players build campuses.

Version 1 Regions:

- North America & Caribbean
- South America
- Europe
- Asia
- Africa
- Oceania

---

## Competition Circuits

Competition circuits determine where countries compete.

Examples include:

- Regional Championships
- World Championships
- Olympic Games
- Winter Championships
- Football World Championship
- World Baseball Classic
- Rugby World Championship
- Basketball World Cup

A country belongs to one geographic region but may participate in multiple competition circuits.

# Headquarters

Headquarters is the heart of the Global Academy.

It manages:

- Research
- Coach Education
- Operations
- Global Funding
- Expansion
- Future Systems

---

# World Tour

Countries are permanent campuses.

Players may freely return to previously developed campuses.

Each campus maintains its own identity, facilities, athletes, competitions, and progression.

# Success Criteria

Version 1 is successful if:

Players become attached to their academy.

Every region feels unique.

Every country has a clear identity.

Players enjoy returning to previous campuses.

Competition circuits create meaningful long-term goals.

Headquarters gives purpose to global expansion.

Olympic Games feel like a major achievement rather than the end of the game.

# Guiding Philosophy

Players should never feel like they are clearing levels.

Players should feel like they are building a global sports organization.

Every campus should have a reason to visit.

Every sport should have a place to shine.

Every competition should feel meaningful.

The journey toward Olympic greatness should be as rewarding as winning the Games themselves.
