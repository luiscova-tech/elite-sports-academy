# 🏆 COMPETITIVE_ECOSYSTEM.md

# Purpose

Defines how countries improve, compete, and create a living sports world.

---

# Philosophy

The world evolves alongside the player.

Every save creates its own sports history.

No country begins with historical championships.

History is created during gameplay.

---

# Core Gameplay Loop

Research

↓

Develop Athletes

↓

Compete

↓

Analyze Results

↓

Improve

↓

Repeat

---

# Country Identity

Each country develops independently.

Countries improve through:

- Facilities
- Research
- Coaching
- Youth Development
- Competition Experience

The player chooses where to invest.

---

# Sport Independence

Every sport has its own history.

Example:

Japan

Baseball ★★★★

Football ★

Swimming ★★

Each sport tracks its own champions.

---

# Championship Stars

Winning a World Championship adds one permanent star to that country's shield for that sport.

Stars are displayed only while viewing that sport.

Example

Baseball

🇯🇵 ★★★★

🇺🇸 ★★★

🇩🇴 ★★

Football

🇧🇷 ★★★★★

🇪🇸 ★★

Stars never carry between sports.

---

# Living World

Countries continue improving even if the player ignores them.

Major nations remain competitive.

Smaller nations can become powers over time.

Player actions influence the balance of the world.

---

# History

Every sport records:

- Champions
- Runner-up
- Third Place
- Host Nation
- MVP
- Biggest Upset
- World Rankings

History is permanent for the save file.

---

# Goal

The player should feel like they are participating in a living sports world rather than isolated competitions.

---

# Tournament Structure

Every major competition follows a complete tournament format.

Possible stages include:

- Qualification
- Preliminary Round
- Group Stage
- Round of 16
- Quarterfinal
- Semifinal
- Third Place Match
- Championship Final

Not every competition uses every stage.

Smaller competitions may begin directly with knockout rounds.

---

# Qualification

Qualification depends on the competition.

Examples:

- Continental Ranking
- Previous Championship Results
- Host Nation
- Wild Card Qualification

Some countries may fail to qualify.

Qualification should feel like an achievement.

---

# Group Stage

Teams are divided into groups.

Each match matters.

Standings include:

- Wins
- Losses
- Draws (where applicable)
- Points
- Point Differential

Top teams advance.

---

# Knockout Stage

Single elimination.

Every match can eliminate a country.

Winning advances.

Losing ends the tournament.

---

# Tournament Difficulty

Difficulty is determined by:

- Country strength in that sport
- Current athlete quality
- Coaching
- Facilities
- Research
- Recent performance

Every tournament should feel different because countries continue improving over time.

---

# Scouting Opponents

Before every match the player can review:

- Opponent ranking
- Championship stars
- Recent results
- Key strengths
- Weaknesses

Preparation should matter as much as training.

---

# Goal

Every tournament should tell a story.

Winning should feel earned.

Losing should reveal what needs to improve before the next attempt.

---

# World Rankings

Each sport maintains its own independent world rankings.

Rankings update after every sanctioned competition.

Rankings determine:

- Tournament seeding
- Qualification
- Rivalries
- Prestige

A country may rank #1 in Baseball while ranking #42 in Football.

---

# Rivalries

Countries naturally develop rivalries over time.

Examples:

- Frequent championship meetings
- Historic upsets
- Consecutive finals
- Regional rivals

Rivalries should appear in match previews and statistics.

---

# Dynasties

If a country wins multiple championships within a short period, it becomes a Dynasty.

Examples

Baseball

🇯🇵 Japan
World Champions
2028
2030
2032
2034

Status

Current Dynasty

Dynasties increase prestige but also increase expectations.

Ending a dynasty should become a memorable achievement.

---

# Historic Upsets

The game records major upsets.

Examples

Largest Ranking Upset

#42 Ghana defeated #1 Japan

2036 World Baseball Classic Quarterfinal

Historic upsets become part of the permanent save history.

---

# Legacy

Every save should tell a different story.

Some saves may be remembered for Japanese baseball dominance.

Others may remember Ghana becoming a baseball powerhouse.

The player is creating an alternate sports history, not replaying the real one.

---

# World News

The world generates news throughout every season.

News helps the player understand what is happening outside their own academy.

Examples:

- Championship Results
- Major Upsets
- New World Rankings
- Rivalries
- Dynasty Updates
- Qualification Results
- Record Performances
- Hall of Fame Inductions

---

# Headlines

Examples

🇯🇵 Japan Wins Fifth Straight Baseball World Championship

🇬🇭 Ghana Eliminates USA in Historic Upset

🇧🇷 Brazil Returns to #1 in World Football Rankings

🇸🇮 Slovenia Continues Cycling Dominance

🇫🇷 France Ends Spain's 18-Match Winning Streak

---

# Seasonal Awards

Every sport concludes with annual awards.

Examples

- Player of the Year
- Coach of the Year
- Breakthrough Nation
- Biggest Upset
- Match of the Year
- Team of the Year

Awards become part of the permanent history.

---

# Records

Each sport tracks historical records.

Examples

- Most Championships
- Most Consecutive Championships
- Longest Winning Streak
- Highest World Ranking
- Most Olympic Gold Medals
- Largest Upset
- Most Decorated Athlete
- Most Decorated Coach

Records may be broken at any time.

---

# Living Timeline

Every season is added to the world's timeline.

A player should be able to scroll back years later and see how the world evolved.

The timeline becomes the history of that save file.
